Joseph Ritchie is currently the Graduate Teaching Assistant for the tuba and euphonium ​studio at Illinois State University.

Joseph received a Bachelor's degree in Music Education with a Performance Certificate from Winthrop University in Rock Hill, South Carolina, and is currently pursuing a Master of Music degree in Tuba Performance from Illinois State University in Normal, Illinois.

Joseph has performed concertos with the Winthrop University Wind Symphony, Winthrop Symphonic Band, and Carolina Youth Symphony.

In 2018, Joseph was a finalist in the SERTEC Artist Solo Competition, and 1st Place in the MARTEC Young Artist Solo Competition and Mock Orchestra Competition. In 2017, he was Honorable Mention in the SCMTA Young Artist Solo Competition, and 3rd Place in the SERTEC Young Artist Solo Competition. In 2016, he was Runner-Up in the National MTNA Senior Brass Competition.

During the summer of 2016, Joseph was a performer in the show Mix It Up! Italian Style at Busch Gardens in Williamsburg, Virginia.

Joseph has performed with the CBDNA Small Band Program, Carolina Wind Orchestra, Winthrop Wind Symphony, Winthrop Symphonic Band, Winthrop Orchestra, and Carolina Youth Symphony. Joseph is also a substitute tubist for the Rock Hill Symphony.

Joseph has taught private-lessons throughout York County in SC. Joseph has had multiple students placed in state and regional level ensembles, and helped his students receive numerous collegiate music scholarships. Joseph was previously the low brass instructor at Clover High School in Clover, SC, and Charlotte Latin Schools in Charlotte, NC.

Joseph's primary teachers include Dr. Andy Rummel, Dr. Doug Black, and Mr. John Sizemore. Joseph has also studied under Wes Lebo and Wesley Day.

He currently resides in Bloomington, Illinois, with his wife, Nicholle. ​
